REBECCA CLOSE

(b. London) works at the intersection of art, technology, critical pedagogy and poetry. Their films, workshops, poems, ceramics and creative coding experiments often address everyday experiences of the Internet interface, notions of the network and the viral as these intersect and inform social relationships, sexuality, the familial and the commons.

They are the author of two poetry collections: Valid, Virtual, Vegetable Reality (Melita Hume Prize, 2017) and Réplica (Canal, 2022) and edit Them, All Magazine for poetry and Net/Software/Code art.

Their research work considers the poetic and political dimensions of science, technology and medicine, with a focus on reproduction, and has been supported by residencies with (counter)institutions, including the Center for Art, Design + Social Research (2017-2019) and Hangar.org (2018). Their doctoral research project on post-internet reproductive politics was awarded the 2024 School of Arts, Design and Architecture Doctoral Thesis Award at Aalto University. They were a Visiting Scholar at ReproSoc, Cambridge University, in 2022.

They studied on the Independent Studies Program at MACBA, Barcelona, (2012) and have since formed part of different collectives, including Criticaldías, whose projects center experimental archiving, collective performance and voice practices. They have lectured in Critical Theory and Experimental Practices, and have given classes and workshops on poetic and artistic ways of knowing for adults and young people at many universities and independent arts and activist spaces.
