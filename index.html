REBECCA CLOSE

works at the intersection of art, new media and poetry, with a research focus on reproduction and reproductive politics. Their films, ceramic pieces, creative coding experiments and poems often address everyday experiences of the Internet interface, notions of the network and the viral as these intersect and inform social relationships, sexuality, experiences of the familial and the commons.

They are the author of two poetry collections: Valid, Virtual, Vegetable Reality (Melita Hume Prize, 2018) and Réplica (Canal, 2022). Their short film The Wife of Them All premiered at the 27th LesGaiCineMad Festival, VAFT Festival Turku, Grrlhauscinema Berlin, and travelled to the 69th International Oberhausen Short Film Festival with support from HAMACA moving image platform.

Their research work has been supported through fellowships from the Center for Art, Design + Social Research, a residency with Hangar.org, Casa Velázquez and a Kone Foundation grant to support their doctoral research project “Post-Internet Queer Reproductive Work and the Fixed Capital of Fertility” at Aalto University, Finland. They were a lecturer in New Media at The University of the Arts, Guayaquil, Ecuador (2015-2016) and have given classes and workshops on poetic and artistic practice at many universities and independent arts and activist spaces over the last decade. They are one-half of artist duo Criticaldías, who have done numerous curatorial and artistic projects that center feminist and anticolonial experimental archiving practices. They edit and design Them, All Magazine for poetry and Net/Software/Code art on reproductive politics and sexuality.
