REBECCA CLOSE

works at the intersection of art, new media and poetry, with a research focus on histories of reproduction and reproductive technologies. Their films, ceramic pieces, creative coding experiments and poems often address everyday experiences of the Internet interface, notions of the network and the viral as these intersect and inform social relationships, sexuality, experiences of the familial and access to the commons.

They are the author of two poetry collections: Valid, Virtual, Vegetable Reality (Melita Hume Prize, 2018) and Réplica (Canal, 2022). Their poems and criticism has appeared in Ambit, Magma, Bath Magg, Frieze, Art Radar, Review 31, Editorial Concreta, NO NIIN, and Art Asia Pacific Magazine, where they are a desk editor. Their projects have been supported by a fellowship from the Center for Art, Design + Social Research, a residency with Hangar and Casa Velázquez and a Kone Foundation grant to support their doctoral project “Post-Internet Queer Reproductive Work and the Fixed Capital of Fertility” at Aalto University.

They have a Foundation Diploma in Art & Design from Arts Institute Bournemouth, a degree in Philosophy from Manchester University and studied under Paul B. Preciado at the Independent Studies Program. They have been based in Barcelona since the early 2010s, where they contributed to various art and poetry collectives and have since taught New Media, Artistic research and critical theory and gender studies at The University of the Arts, Ecuador; Aalto University, Finland; and University of Barcelona Autonoma, Spain. They are one-half of BCN-based duo Criticaldías, who have done numerous curatorial and artistic projects on art and experimental archiving practices. They are the editor and designer of Them, All Magazine for poetry and Net/Software/Code art on reproductive politics.
