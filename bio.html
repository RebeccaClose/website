REBECCA CLOSE

works at the intersection of art, new media and poetry, with a research focus on reproduction and reproductive politics. Their films, ceramic pieces, creative coding experiments and poems often address everyday experiences of the Internet interface, notions of the network and the viral as these intersect and inform social relationships, sexuality, experiences of the familial and the commons.

They are the author of two poetry collections: Valid, Virtual, Vegetable Reality (Melita Hume Prize, 2018) and Réplica (Canal, 2022). Their short film The Wife of Them All premiered at the 27th LesGaiCineMad Festival and travelled to the 69th International Oberhausen Short Film Festival with support from HAMACA moving image platform.

Their work has been supported recently through a fellowship with the Center for Art, Design + Social Research (2017-2019), a residency with Hangar & Casa Velazquez (2018) and a Kone Foundation grant to support doctoral research at Aalto University, Finland (2019-2023). They were a Lecturer in Critical Theory and Experimental Practices at The University of the Arts Guayaquil, Ecuador (2015-2016), and have given classes and workshops at many universities and independent arts and activist spaces. They are one-half of arts duo Criticaldías, whose curatorial projects and collective performances center feminist and anticolonial experimental archiving and voice practices. They edit and design Them, All Magazine for poetry and Net/Software/Code art.
